## Concrete project ideas by sponsor and track

Below are **suggested ideas** you can adapt. Each one is designed to (1) fit a track, (2) use the sponsor’s tech, and (3) be realistic to build as a Gradio Space.

### OpenBMB (MiniCPM) ideas

- **Backyard AI – Local-language helper for a nearby business**
Build a **MiniCPM-based multilingual assistant** that helps a local shop (e.g. café, barbershop, or small restaurant near you) handle messages: translate customer questions, draft social posts, and summarize reviews, all in German and English (and possibly Turkish or other local languages).[^1]
    - Why it wins: Real, measurable impact for a specific owner; uses a small OpenBMB LLM; easy to demo in Gradio.[^1]
- **Thousand Token Wood – Personalized story machine for a kid you know**
Use MiniCPM to create a **“bedtime story tailor”**: the parent enters kid’s name, favorite things, and the tool generates a short illustrated story each night (you can plug in a small image model later).[^1]
    - Why it wins: Clearly uses AI for creativity; uses MiniCPM; has emotional, shareable demos (good for community choice too).[^1]


### Cohere ideas (Tiny Aya, Transcribe)

- **Backyard AI – Voice-to-multilingual assistant for immigrants / expats**
An app where someone nearby (e.g. a neighbor whose German is not strong) can speak in their language; **Cohere Transcribe** converts speech to text, and **Tiny Aya** translates and simplifies official letters or forms into their native language.[^4][^1]
    - Strong story: truly helpful, easy to get feedback from one real person, strongly aligned with Cohere’s recommended use.[^1]
- **Thousand Token Wood – Multilingual improv storytelling game**
A Gradio app where multiple users speak lines in different languages; Transcribe + Tiny Aya turn it into a chaotic, multilingual fairy tale shared on screen.[^4][^1]
    - Fun, chaotic, clearly requires AI, and uses Cohere’s recommended models.


### JetBrains ideas (developer tooling)

- **Backyard AI – Local freelancer coding assistant for a specific dev**
Build a **MiniCPM or Tiny Aya–powered code review helper** tuned to one freelance developer you know: it reads their Git diffs, comments in a “JetBrains-like” panel inside the Gradio app, and suggests tests or refactors.[^1]
    - Pitch it as a “mock JetBrains plugin” — your README explains how it would integrate into PyCharm, IntelliJ, etc.[^1]
- **Thousand Token Wood – Gamified bug-hunting arcade**
A small game: paste in code, the AI hides “bugs” and you have to find/fix them for points; JetBrains theming, animations, and scoreboard.[^1]
    - Good for JetBrains, fun for judges, and easy to build with a small LLM for code analysis.


### Black Forest Labs ideas (FLUX.2-klein)

- **Backyard AI – Local business poster \& menu generator**
Use **FLUX.2-klein** plus a tiny LLM to create on-brand posters, flyers, or social images for a real nearby shop (café, kebab place, etc.), with simple text prompts.[^1]
    - Very visual before/after, obvious value to the owner, and uses Black Forest’s models.[^1]
- **Thousand Token Wood – “Dream postcards” from your neighborhood**
Users type moods and locations (e.g. “Neckar at sunset but cyberpunk”), and FLUX.2-klein generates stylized postcards; you might fine-tune a LoRA on a small custom photo set.[^1]
    - Great fit for creative track and for Black Forest’s art-centric positioning.


### NVIDIA ideas (Nemotron / GPU)

- **Backyard AI – Smart queue + recommendation screen for a local café**
Use a small NeMo/Nemotron model to recommend menu items on a digital screen: given time of day, weather, and sales history, it suggests daily specials and short descriptions.[^3][^1]
    - Argue that GPU acceleration lets it respond in real time, with nice visual charts in the Gradio app.
- **Thousand Token Wood – GPU-accelerated AI NPC chatbot**
An “AI bartender” or “AI museum guide” in a tiny game world, powered by a small NVIDIA model running on an RTX GPU; interactions displayed in a playful Gradio UI.[^1]
    - Strong Nemotron Quest candidate + fun to play with.


### Modal ideas (deployment story)

- **Backyard AI – Multi-step assistant for a busy local shop**
Build a **pipeline on Modal**: OCR receipts → summarize inventory → suggest restock orders → send weekly email drafts, all triggered from your Gradio frontend.[^1]
    - Judges see a full production-style workflow, nicely showing Modal’s value.
- **Thousand Token Wood – “AI orchestration” playground**
A small toy that chains several tiny models via Modal (e.g. text LLM + image model + speech) to generate an AI “concert” or visual-novel scene.[^1]
    - Good story for Modal: multiple services orchestrated in the cloud.


### OpenAI Codex ideas

- **Backyard AI – Coding assistant for a specific non-expert**
A Codex-based assistant tailored to, say, your roommate who’s learning Python: they describe what they want, Codex writes code, and the app adds explanations in simple language.[^1]
    - Make sure Codex does real work (commits in Git), and the README highlights that Codex is central.[^1]
- **Thousand Token Wood – Code-as-art generator**
Users describe a “code sculpture” (like ASCII art animations, generative music scripts), Codex writes the code, and the app runs and visualizes it in-browser.[^1]
    - Very on-brand for Codex + obviously creative.
